Project Details

During the transition to a new ERP system, I collaborated with the Data Analyst to design a simplified ERD that would make order data easier to analyze and interpret.

Building an ERD for Analyst Needs (Perpack ERP System)

This project is based on a real challenge I faced as a Database Administrator in a fast-paced retail environment. Our company had just onboarded a new ERP system called Perpack, which recently extended its capabilities to include sales operations. When the system switched from legacy to Perpack, the Data Analyst team needed access to the sales data structure in order to start building dashboards and reporting pipelines via Metabase. The issue?

There was no existing ERD (Entity Relationship Diagram) provided, and internal constraints made it impossible to request one directly from the ERP developers. o I stepped in, and did what any problem-solving DBA would do.

My Role

Although this wasn’t part of my original job description, I took the initiative to:

  1. Coordinate with the Data Analyst to understand the core tables they needed (primarily orders)
  2. Analyze the orders table, which contained nearly 100 columns, to identify:
    • Primary foreign keys
    • Relevant entities connected to the order workflow
  3. Bridge communication between Sales leadership and ERP dev team (since our direct leader didn’t support the request)
  4. Build a custom ERD to visualize the data flow clearly for analytics purposes

ERD Structure I Designed

I categorized the entire data flow into three main pillars:

  • Sales & Transactional Info – anything related to the order lifecycle
  • User Entities – identifying who’s involved (e.g., admin, customers, the role)
  • Warehouse Locations – linking order flows to physical stock and shipping routes

From just one main table (orders), I reverse-engineered all its foreign key connections and mapped out related tables to clarify how the sales data flows across the ERP.

Results

  • Built a fully usable ERD for the orders module
  • Helped the Data Analyst start querying cleanly for Metabase dashboarding
  • Reduced dependency on ERP vendor for future table clarification
  • Positioned myself as a go-to for internal data infrastructure questions

Takeaway

"Sometimes, being a DBA isn’t about writing SQL, it’s also about seeing the gap, asking the right questions, and building the thing that no one else will."

This project reminded me that solid data systems only happen when we put collaboration first, even when support doesn’t come from where we expect.
