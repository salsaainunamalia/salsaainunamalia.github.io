Building an ERD for Analyst Needs (Perpack ERP System)

Pasar Jaya Asem Reges is a semi-modern market located in the heart of the city, functioning as a vital economic hub for the local community. With hundreds of shop units (ruko) available for rent, the market supports various traders, from basic necessities to electronics sellers, while also contributing to employment and the local economy.

However, rental management at the market was still conducted manually. This included tenant data recording, rent payment scheduling, and lease documentation—mostly handled through physical books and paper documents.

This manual system led to several issues:

  • Frequent data entry errors
  • Delayed information processing
  • Difficulty accessing critical data quickly
  • Lack of integration and operational inefficiency

All of these contributed to reduced satisfaction for tenants and slowed down the market's administration process.

Objective

To address these challenges, I designed a rental management information system aimed at automating shop rental operations at Pasar Jaya Asem Reges. The system was intended to:

  • Digitize tenant registration and lease contracts
  • Track payment status and rental periods
  • Improve data accuracy and access
  • Speed up administrative workflows

In the end, the project aimed to support a more effective and efficient market management process—laying the foundation for digital transformation in a traditional environment.

My Role

In this internship project, I was responsible for several core components of the system development:

  • Designing the database structure, including table relationships and data normalization
  • Creating UI mockups that reflect user needs and process flow
  • Modeling system architecture using UML diagrams such as use case, activity, and class diagrams
  • Collaborating with a team of 7 to compile the final project report, ensuring technical clarity and proper documentation

Through this role, I gained experience in aligning technical design with real business requirements and contributing to a structured team-based development process.

Chalenge & solutions

During our internship project, my team of seven had to navigate differences in technical capabilities, task delegation, and time management while working on a real client case. I was responsible for designing the database structure, building the UI layout, creating UML diagrams, and contributing to the final report alongside my teammates.

One of the main challenges was adapting our academic knowledge to real-world conditions. Since the market's operations were still managed manually, we had to gather information directly from staff and adjust our system design accordingly. This process helped us build a solution that was both functional and relevant to the company's workflow.

Takeaway

"This project helped me see how even the most manual systems can be improved with structured thinking, good design, and teamwork."

It was also a valuable experience in translating real-world operational problems into a working digital solution—while learning how to document and communicate those ideas clearly across a team.
